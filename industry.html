Simplifications & assumptions (v1)
  • Inputs are priced DEPTH-AWARE against the condensed Jita book (top 40 levels/side): buying walks the sell levels for the full batch quantity; instant selling walks the buy levels respecting each order's min-volume. A book that runs dry prices the remainder at the worst listed level and flags the row/node with ⚠ instead of zeroing it. Invention consumables (datacores, decryptors) stay at the scalar top quote.
  • Market fees come from THREE role characters: the buyer's broker % is added on top of input buy orders, the seller's sales tax + broker hit revenue, and the manufacturer's skills drive job times, invention, slots, the skilled filter and owned-blueprint merge ties.
  • Everything is priced at BATCH scale: R runs/job × J parallel jobs, bounded by invented-BPC runs or the blueprint's run limit, a ~24h soft job cap, your job slots (1 + Mass Production + Advanced Mass Production etc.), the demand cap and your capital. The batch prices as ONE aggregate job — per-job material ceilings can differ by up to J−1 units per material.
  • ISK/h and Profit/Day are steady-state PIPELINE numbers: profit/unit × the slowest stage rate (manufacturing/reaction, copying, invention — science slots are pooled proportionally between copying and invention). Intermediate build jobs do NOT consume top-level slots in v1, and reaction formulas are assumed available for every parallel job.
  • The full-market scan plans with demandPerDay = null (ranking can't wait for thousands of history fetches); rows whose history is loaded get their batch re-planned against real demand as they become visible, marked ↺.
  • Shipping splits into ceil(m³ / max haul) courier contracts per direction, each paying the base + its own round-up; cargo and collateral split evenly across hauls. Per-item shipping amortizes the whole batch.
  • Invention consumables are amortized into cost but NOT added to the inbound shipping haul.
  • Demand/history is regional (The Forge) and mixes both order sides — ESI has nothing narrower.
  • A facility's product scope is applied per END PRODUCT: the whole tree of that product builds at the facilities its scope selects; intermediates are not re-scoped mid-tree.
  • Owned-blueprint ME/TE applies to the end product's own blueprint; intermediate blueprints use the "unowned BPO" defaults above. Owned T2 BPCs are shown in the BP column but priced via invention.
  • Structure role bonuses and rig values are hardcoded presets (marked "verify in game") — override them per facility if your numbers differ.
